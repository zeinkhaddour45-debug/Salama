Mental Health Hub

AUB offers a range of mental health and wellbeing resources for students, faculty, and staff. Below is an overview of the services available to you on campus.

Office locations and contact numbers may change over time. Visit each unit's website for the most up-to-date information.

Available Services

Resources on Campus

Counseling Center

Free

Provides short-term psychological support and crisis intervention at no cost to AUB students. The goal is to help students build awareness, independence, and healthier daily habits. Appointments can be booked through the online calendar system and are offered on a first-come, first-serve basis.

Learn more →

University Health Services (UHS)

HIP coverage may apply

UHS family physicians are trained to identify mental health concerns, prescribe medications when appropriate, and provide ongoing follow-up. If specialist care is needed, they can refer students to AUBMC psychologists or psychiatrists.

HIP members: If you are enrolled in AUB's Health Insurance Plan, you have mental health coverage for both inpatient and outpatient care. Terms and conditions apply. Contact the benefits office to learn more about your coverage.
Learn more →

Department of Psychiatry

Referral may apply

Students, faculty, and staff can access psychiatric and psychological care through the Department of Psychiatry. Those referred by UHS are seen as part of regular care. If you are not a HIP member, you can also contact the department directly. Services include outpatient visits, Emergency Department access, and inpatient care when necessary.

Learn more →

Employee Assistance Program (EAP)

Free · No referral needed

AUB employees can access free, confidential counseling through the Employee Health Department. Sessions are led by trained psychologists from the AUBMC Department of Psychiatry. No referral is required to get started.

Learn more →

Office of Equal Opportunity & Title IX

Support Office

This office coordinates AUB's response to discrimination and discriminatory harassment, including sex discrimination and sexual harassment. If you have experienced or witnessed any form of misconduct, this office is your point of contact.

Learn more →

Accessible Education Office

Accommodations

If you have a mental, physical, or psychological disability and need accommodations in your courses or programs, reach out to the Accessible Education Office. AUB is committed to fostering an inclusive university community that welcomes and supports people of all backgrounds and abilities.

Learn more →

Emergency

In case of an emergency

Please head to the ER Department immediately. If you need an ambulance on campus, call ext. 7777.
