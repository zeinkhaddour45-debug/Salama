Community-Based Services

Grounded in the belief that communities have the capacity for recovery and resilience, Restart Center's community-based programs mobilize local resources and empower individuals to support one another.

IASC Intervention Pyramid

Level of Intervention

Focused, Non-Specialized Supports

  • Community-based psychosocial support, co-facilitated by trained and supervised (refugee) outreach volunteers.
  • Psychological First Aid (Beirut Blast Program).
  • Basic mental health care provided by primary health care workers.

Foundation

The foundation of all community-based psychosocial work is the belief in the affected community's capacity for recovery and resilience. All communities and individuals have resources and strategies for dealing with difficulties, illness, and distress.

Approach

Key Points & Approach

Key Points

  • Community-based psychosocial support builds on individual and community resources, capacities, and resilience.
  • Mobilization of community support networks is an essential element of a sustainable psychosocial response.

Approach

  • Mapping and building on local resources, including community networks, practices, processes, and mechanisms.
  • Mobilizing and training community-level volunteers linked to existing networks and a referral system, and promoting peer support.
  • Meaningful participation of local actors and community members at all stages of engagement.
  • Use of existing entry points such as community centers, social development centers, and other community-based platforms.

Specific Objectives

  • To prevent the onset of mental disorders among people with psychosocial and other mental health problems from refugee populations and host communities.
  • To build the capacity of (refugee) outreach volunteers to design, implement, and evaluate psychosocial support programs in their communities or country of origin in case of safe return.

Process

CB-PSS Three-Month Cycle

I

Identification of Outreach Volunteers

Identifying outreach volunteers with health, education, or other relevant backgrounds.

II

Capacity Building for Outreach Volunteers

  • Screening and referrals of people with mental health and psychosocial problems.
  • Designing, facilitating, and evaluating CB-PSS sessions.
  • Codes of conduct and ethical guidelines.
III

Identification of People in Need

Trained outreach volunteers identify people in need within targeted communities.

IV

Group Participatory Assessment Sessions

Group sessions targeting identified affected persons to understand their needs, resources, and gaps, and to develop the main objectives for the CB-PSS program.

V

Needs Assessments & Registration

Individual needs assessments and registration of affected persons.

VI

Formation of CB-PSS Groups

Groups of 10 to 15 participants formed based on age, gender, vulnerabilities, culture, geographical area, and needs.

VII

Development of Tailored Programs

A tailored CB-PSS program developed for each group based on participatory assessment findings and individual needs.

VIII

Implementation

Around 10 to 15 remote or face-to-face sessions per group, facilitated by Restart Center specialists and co-facilitated by trained outreach volunteers.

IX

Program Evaluation

Structured evaluation of the CB-PSS program's effectiveness and outcomes.

Program Types

Types of CBS Programs

  • Psychosocial support
  • Peace-building education
  • Awareness-raising
  • Parental guidance targeting parents of children with special needs
  • Physiotherapy
  • Recreational activities including cultural and artistic performances

Aim of PSS Activities

  • Foster social connections and interactions through existing community networks of children, youth, and women.
  • Normalize daily life.
  • Promote a sense of competence and restoration of control over one's life.
  • Build on and encourage individual and community resilience to crisis.
  • Provide for identifying, referring, and treating individuals with severe mental disorders.
  • Strengthen social cohesion between host communities and refugee populations.

Who They Serve

Target Groups

  • Survivors of torture, violence, and trauma
  • Survivors of gender-based violence
  • Persons deprived of their liberty in places of detention
  • Refugees
  • Children with special needs and their parents/caregivers
  • Persons with mental illness
  • Women and children
  • Women heads of households
  • Vulnerable individuals from the LGBT community
  • Children with emotional or behavioral problems (including in public schools and orphanages)
